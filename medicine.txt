If you're experiencing mild symptoms such as a headache, mild pain, or a common cold, some general over-the-counter medications often used include:

1. **Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)**: Often used for pain relief and fever reduction.
   - **Brands**: Tylenol, Panadol, Calpol.
   - **Side effects**: Generally well-tolerated, but high doses can cause liver damage.

2. **Ibuprofen**: Used for pain relief and anti-inflammatory purposes.
   - **Brands**: Advil, Motrin, Nurofen.
   - **Side effects**: Can cause stomach upset, and long-term use may lead to kidney damage or increased risk of heart attack.

3. **Antihistamines (e.g., cetirizine, loratadine)**: For allergy symptoms.
   - **Brands**: Zyrtec, Claritin.
   - **Side effects**: May cause drowsiness, dry mouth, or dizziness.

Again, it's critical to follow the dosage instructions provided on the packaging and consult a healthcare provider before starting any medication. If your symptoms persist or worsen, seek medical attention promptly.
